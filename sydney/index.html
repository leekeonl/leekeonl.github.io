My Portfolio

This semester I completed the course EDUC 326: Making Positive Learning Communities and am now leaving the course with a new outlook on what makes a classroom environment ideal for student growth, learning, and success. While I am an aspiring mathematics teacher and came into the course with many ideas on how I would like to guide my future students, I certainly learned a lot! The activities in this course gave me a means to learn new ways to create my desired learning environment, as well as reflect upon the ways I already had in mind. I often found myself being asked to investigate ways in which I learn best and actions which I take to fulfill my own needs. I explored the importance of choice in my life and my preferences when it comes to learning type. All these aspects of myself proved very beneficial to explore, as my students will also be unique individuals with their own needs, desires, and choices. I was reminded how much teachers can learn from their students and the importance of creating positive and trusting relationships with my students wherein everyone has an equal input. I was also reminded to be conscious of my students’ feelings of safety and comfort, and claim responsibility for the running of my class, whether a task is successful or not. My roster of techniques for creating my desired learning community has certainly grown after completing the course and I am excited to share what I have learned. My portfolio encompasses my Week 4 peer editing task, as well as four other excerpts from activities and assignments that I handpicked because I believe they all represent pinnacles of my learning throughout the course. I also provided short reasonings as to why I made the choice to include each snippet.
