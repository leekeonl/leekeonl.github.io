Reflection for Module1

I really enjoyed module 1 for educ 326. At the beginning of the class, there are many interesting questions inspire me to find out my passion in teaching, I like sharing my happiness with our group and listen to their stories. As the class progressed, I was identify myself more clearly, which I was able to find the type(characteristic) of person I belong to and how to get communicate with different type of people. These were important for me because these assignments help me to learn little things from doing assignment. Also if I were teacher show me how I deal with this kind of special situations when I become a teacher, and I can give wise solution based on an what I learn from this class. Each time we exchange ideas in a discussion group, I can learn a lot of from others, since different kinds of personalities will give different reactions and these can help me keep making progress, particularly, I was able to change my thought on giving hard assignment. The importance of understanding every individual has a different way of learning and perceiving things allows me to prepare myself better teacher. The narrative about a teacher that makes me angry keep reminding me of avoiding to do the same things as them, which I will try to negotiate as I can. I hope to provide a safe, supportive environment for my students in the future. The special needs part of this module has allowed me to identify different ways to accommodate these students. All the peer-interaction tasks, peer editing assignments in this module were very useful, and I think that when I look back on these assignments. I can reflect back to the time when all these were hypothetical scenarios that I had in my head such as deducting marks from late assignments, laugh about it, and apply them into my current class. They will always remind me, encourage me and support me. After all, I know why I want to be a great math teacher at this moment.

Reflection for Module2

In module 2, our class were to think about the meaning of being educated. As learning is a life-long journey, the role of being a good teacher is really important. During working on progress log, I was able to know myself better, and know what kind of teacher I am always dreaming about to be, also the environment I want to provide them in my own classroom. These are rewarding since the answers are like a goal which will always remind me of being a model of students. Meanwhile, constructing the credo is another important part during this module. After I wrote down credo, I had no more hesitation and be confident about my teaching life. If I believe I could do it, I will make it. The effectiveness of evaluation is another important part of this module. I learned how it can impact a student, and what we should avoid in the evaluations 1. Measuring what is easiest to measure, 2. Underestimating the Learning Embedded in Evaluation 3. Reduction 4. Examined Power 5. Ineffective Feedback 6. Ignoring Individual Differences I have learned that evaluation should be thought of a teaching process, only teacher evaluate students’ performances and give feedback on time, you can see the value of evaluation on them. Besides, it’s a powerful teaching tool since the purpose of it is to “catch the doing the right thing”. The entire classroom atmosphere will change along with the effective of evaluation.

Reflection for Module3

In module 3, it focuses more on the relationship between the environment and our behavior. First, the task reminds us a time when we “wronged” people. During the process of coming up with a plan for restitution, I take time to think about my own part. Also I realized my fault when I discuss with others. This is helpful because I will also encounter various difficulties when I communicate with students in the future. In order to be a good teacher, I need to negotiate with them and come up with the best solution for each conversation. I need to avoid those shortages in the above experiences, and tie the action to a higher value. As for the ten characteristics of effective classroom rules, I just did not memorize something but also understand the detail and importance of each rule, which is crucial for keeping a positive environment in my classroom. There are ten rules, each of them has a reason to be there, and I explain them carefully during progress log. In the assignment3, after I observe the performance of boys and girls in the class, and relate it to my own experience, I reach at the conclusion that Pipher’s work does not appeal to the classrooms today. Gender equity is better promoted now rather than the prototypes such as “boys are better at math” and “girls are better at badminton” idea. I was able to improve myself with discussions with other classmates. I could always get new thoughts and perspectives, I am happy that I am able to grab these concepts and hope to adapt them to my own classroom one day.
